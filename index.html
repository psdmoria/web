The project

Program for the sustainable development of Lesbos is a project that aims to understand the past and the present situation of the island in order to propose new measures to change the uncertain future it currently faces. The project seeks to offer an overview of the territory and its population years after the migratory crisis as a fundamental part for the reflection and evaluation of the actions done and the changes happened on the physical and social environment of the area. The main objective is to encourage proposals and interventions addressed both the refugee and local communities, looking for an ecosystem balance in order to reduce the consequences on the local economy but not forgetting to give a response to the present and future needs of the refugees.

Goals

#1

Ensure equal access to quality public services.

#2

Diversify the sources of the economy and establish stable levels of employment.

#3

Ensuring a sustainable environment by protecting local flora and fauna, one of the main resources of the island.

#4

Build a community where access to shelter and housing is both affordable and universal, for everyone.

About us

We are a group of architects, urban planners, geographers and professionals from different fields with experience in urban, social and territorial development. In view of the great challenge of today's situation on the island of Lesbos, we have created a group that has the aim of providing a comprehensive solution to the current difficulties of the island. This group is based in two locations, one in Barcelona and the other one on the island of Lesbos itself. From Barcelona and through the support of the Polytechnic University of Catalonia and the Center for Cooperation and Development, there is access to the resources provided from the university and also to the large staff of academics and experts in their respective fields who work as external advisors. In the case of the Lesbos office, the work carried out is field research work. From here, we are in direct contact with public institutions, local population and other entities, whether private or non-profit, which makes it easier to collect data and information.
